Low Voltage Residential Electrical Distribution & Lighting Design

1. Project Overview & Scope

The Objective: This project involved the complete electrical systems design for a multi-story residential building (Matrouh Project). The scope encompassed the entire low-voltage distribution lifecycle: from initial lighting simulations and socket placement to circuit routing, panel scheduling, and the final Bill of Quantities (BOQ).

The Engineering Challenge: The primary challenge was to ensure code compliance (NEC/IEC) while optimizing the system for efficiency and cost. This required precise load balancing across phases to prevent neutral current issues, careful voltage drop calculations for distant circuits, and the integration of distinct systems (Lighting, Power, HVAC) into a unified AutoCAD drafting standard.

AutoCAD Layout: Power Distribution & Circuit Routing

2. Technical Design Process

Lighting Design (DIALux Evo)

Lighting was not just placed arbitrarily; it was calculated. Using DIALux, I simulated the lux levels for each zone to meet standard requirements:

  • Reception/Living: Targeted 150-200 Lux for comfort.
  • Kitchens: Targeted 300-500 Lux for task safety.
  • Corridors: 100 Lux for navigation.
  • Process: Selected specific Philips fixtures, ran calculation grids, and exported the finalized layout back to AutoCAD as a reference block.
Power & Distribution (AutoCAD)

The power system was designed to handle diverse residential loads securely.

  • Socket Layout: Placed general purpose outlets (30cm AFF) and kitchen appliance outlets (120cm AFF) based on furniture layouts.
  • HVAC & Heavy Loads: Designed dedicated circuits with isolator switches for AC units and electric water heaters.
  • Circuiting: Grouped devices into logical circuits (e.g., L1, L2, L3) to minimize wire runs and ensure no breaker exceeded 80% capacity.

3. Load Analysis & Panel Scheduling

A critical part of the project was the creation of the Panel Schedule. I utilized Excel to perform the necessary demand load calculations, applying diversity factors to size the main breaker and service feeder cables correctly.

Load Type Connected Load (kVA) Diversity Factor Demand Load (kVA)
Lighting 4.5 kVA 0.66 (First 3kVA @ 100%) 3.5 kVA
Small Power (Sockets) 12.0 kVA 0.40 (NEC Standard) 4.8 kVA
HVAC (AC Units) 18.0 kVA 1.00 (Continuous Load) 18.0 kVA
Water Heaters 6.0 kVA 1.00 6.0 kVA
TOTAL 40.5 kVA - 32.3 kVA

*Result: Selected a 100A 3-Phase MCCB for the Main Distribution Panel (MDP) and sized the feeder cable as 4x35mm² + 16mm² (Earth) based on voltage drop and current carrying capacity tables (Elsewedy Cables).
