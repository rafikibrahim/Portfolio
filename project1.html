Low Voltage Residential Electrical Distribution & Lighting Design

1. Executive Summary & Project Scope

Project Description: The "Matrouh Residential Tower" is a multi-story development consisting of a commercial ground floor and five typical residential floors. This case study focuses on the complete electrical systems design for Level 1 (Typical Floor), which houses five distinct apartment units ranging from 120m² to 180m².

Engineering Objective: The primary goal was to deliver a fully compliant electrical infrastructure adhering to the National Electrical Code (NEC) and IEC 60364 standards. The scope encompassed four critical domains:

  • Photometric Analysis: Ensuring optimal lux levels and uniformity ratios for occupant comfort.
  • Power Distribution: Strategic placement of socket outlets, isolators, and heavy-duty disconnects.
  • HVAC Integration: Sizing feeders and protection for Split and Window AC units.
  • Load Balancing & Protection: Calculating Demand Loads to size the Main Distribution Panel (MDP) and ensuring phase balance within 10%.

3. Technical Methodology & Design Criteria

Photometric Analysis (DIALux Evo)

Lighting design was driven by function rather than aesthetics alone. I established a calculation grid for every room to verify compliance with international standards.

  • Calculation Parameters: Maintenance Factor set to 0.8; Workplane height set to 0.75m.
  • Reception Areas: Targeted 150-200 Lux with a CRI > 80 to ensure a welcoming atmosphere.
  • Kitchens & Workspaces: Targeted 300-500 Lux to ensure safety during food preparation and tasks.
  • Fixture Selection: Utilized Philips CoreLine LED fixtures for high efficiency (100 lm/W) and longevity.
Power Distribution Strategy

The power layout was designed to accommodate modern residential loads while ensuring safety and expandability.

  • General Purpose: 16A Schuko sockets placed every 3 meters along walls (perimeter rule).
  • Heavy Loads: Dedicated 20A and 32A circuits for HVAC units, Water Heaters, and Electric Cookers.
  • Wet Areas: All circuits feeding bathrooms and kitchens are protected by 30mA RCBOs (Residual Current Breaker with Overcurrent).

4. Mathematical Modeling & Load Calculations

Engineering decisions were backed by rigorous calculation. Below is the methodology used to size the Main Distribution Panel (MDP) and the main feeder cables.

1. Voltage Drop Calculation Methodology:
Constraint: V_drop must be < 3% for lighting and < 5% for power.

Formula: Vd = (2 * L * R * I) / 1000
Where:
- L = Length of run (meters)
- R = Resistance of cable (Ohm/km)
- I = Design Current (Amps)

2. Short Circuit Current Estimation:
I_sc = U_o / Z_loop
Result: Standard 6kA Miniature Circuit Breakers (MCBs) were deemed sufficient for all branch circuits based on the utility transformer proximity.
Demand Load Analysis Table

Using standard diversity factors (NEC Table 220.42), the total connected load was reduced to a realistic demand load to optimize the transformer and generator sizing.

Load Category Total Connected Load (VA) Diversity Factor applied Calculated Demand Load (VA)
Lighting (General) 4,500 VA 0.66 (First 3kVA @ 100%, rest @ 35%) 3,500 VA
Small Power (Sockets) 12,000 VA 0.40 (Standard Residential Diversity) 4,800 VA
HVAC (AC Units) 18,000 VA 1.00 (No diversity allowed for HVAC) 18,000 VA
Water Heating 6,000 VA 1.00 (Simultaneous Use Assumed) 6,000 VA
TOTAL SYSTEM LOAD 40,500 VA - 32,300 VA
Feeder Sizing Result

Based on the calculated demand load of 32.3 kVA:

  • Design Current (Ib): 46.5 Amps per phase (3-Phase 400V).
  • Selected Protection (In): 63A 3-Pole MCCB (Molded Case Circuit Breaker).
  • Cable Selection: 4x16mm² Cu/XLPE/PVC + 10mm² Earth (Elsewedy Cables).
  • Derating Factors: Applied grouping factor (0.8) and temperature factor (0.9) for ambient 40°C.
Panel Balancing

Phase balancing was performed to ensure neutral current remains minimal.

  • Red Phase: 10.8 kVA
  • Yellow Phase: 10.7 kVA
  • Blue Phase: 10.8 kVA
  • Imbalance: < 1% (Highly Optimized)
