CanineCore: Interactive Cognitive Trainer

Designed and manufactured "Bark It!", an interactive, sensor-driven game console for canine mental and physical stimulation. The system features three distinct interaction modes—Tug It, Dig It, and Place It—mimicking natural behaviors through embedded hardware[cite: 3, 4, 5].

Engineering Challenges & Solutions

  • Complex Logic Integration: Developed a state-machine in C++ to handle the "Place It" command, requiring a specific sequence of ball removal and placement via limit switches[cite: 65, 69].
  • Optimized PCB Design: Iterated a 2-layer PCB layout using 2-pin connectors to reduce interior wire clutter and improve assembly efficiency[cite: 88, 98, 149].
  • Mechanical Design: Fabricated a durable laser-cut wooden enclosure modeled as a doghouse, featuring a hollow base to house the electronics and cable management systems[cite: 286, 293, 295].

Technical Specifications

  • Controller: ATMega32 Microcontroller.
  • Input Interface: Joystick (Tug It), Push Button (Dig It), and Dual Limit Switches (Place It)[cite: 9, 11, 15].
  • Feedback: LCD real-time score tracking, Piezo-audio module, and dual-LED status indicators[cite: 6, 61, 62].
  • Adaptive Difficulty: Real-time difficulty scaling that reduces task windows as the score increases[cite: 50].
