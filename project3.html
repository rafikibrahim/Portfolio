Retro Multiplayer Game Console - Capstone Senior Design Project

Client: ECE 1896: Senior Design Project

Location: Pittsburgh, PA

Year: 2025

This project involved creating a **dual-screen multiplayer game console** with advanced functionalities, utilizing **embedded systems** and custom **hardware design**. The system runs **classic games** like **Tetris**, **Pong**, and **Minesweeper**, interfacing through **Bluetooth controllers** and delivering high-quality video via **VGA output** to two independent screens. As the **Electrical Engineer**, I spearheaded the design, implementation, and integration of **PCBs**, **power systems**, and **communication protocols**, all while adhering to rigorous engineering constraints and testing standards.

GitHub Repository: You can view the complete project details, including code, schematics, and documentation, on my GitHub repository.

Design Constraints & Challenges

During the design process, we encountered several **engineering challenges** that needed innovative solutions. These are the most notable constraints:

  • Power Management: Achieving **72 hours** of continuous gameplay using **Li-ion batteries** and **DC-DC step-up converters** was one of the most demanding challenges.
  • Low-Latency Bluetooth Communication: Optimizing Bluetooth communication for **real-time player interaction** with minimal lag.
  • Hardware Integration: The complexity of integrating various subsystems (game logic, controller input, video output, and Bluetooth communication) required careful **PCB design**, **signal routing**, and **timing analysis** to ensure smooth operation.
  • Multiplayer Synchronization: Ensuring smooth synchronization of the game states between two players was critical for the gaming experience, which required efficient **UART communication** between two **ESP32 microcontrollers**.

Technical Design

The system is powered by two **ESP32 microcontrollers** that communicate over **UART** for **multiplayer synchronization** and generate the **VGA video output**. Key technical features include:

  • Power System: Utilizes **Li-ion rechargeable batteries** and a **DC-DC step-up converter** for efficient power regulation to ensure stable operation.
  • Bluetooth Communication: **HC-05 Bluetooth modules** are used for **low-latency wireless communication** between the controllers and the main console, ensuring real-time interaction.
  • VGA Output Generation: The **ESP32 microcontroller** generates **VGA signals** to drive two screens with **DMA** techniques to ensure smooth video rendering.
  • Controller Design: The **controller PCB** integrates **ATmega328P microcontrollers**, providing inputs through **joysticks** and **buttons** with wireless communication to the main console via Bluetooth.
